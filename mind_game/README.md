# mind\_game

The mind game honesty task (Jiang, 2013; Kajackaite & Gneezy, 2017). Participants think of a number between 1 and 6, roll an electronic die, and report whether the outcome matched their guess. A correct guess is rewarded with a $100 bonus.

Because the guess is purely mental and never recorded, lying is **individually undetectable** — no one, including the experimenter, can know what number the participant had in mind. Dishonesty can therefore only be inferred at the aggregate level: under truthful reporting, roughly 1/6 (16.7%) of participants should report a correct guess. Any excess above this rate reflects the base rate of lying in the sample.

**References:**

- Jiang, T. (2013). Cheating in mind games: The subtlety of rules matters. *Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization*, 93, 328–336.
- Kajackaite, A., & Gneezy, U. (2017). Incentives and cheating. *Games and Economic Behavior*, 102, 433–444.
