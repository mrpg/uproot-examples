You are part of a small economy with {{ C.GROUP_SIZE }} participants. Together, you produce output from capital and labor. Each period, you will:

  1. Work: Perform a real-effort task to earn labor income. Each correctly solved puzzle counts as one unit of labor (maximum {{ C.L_BAR }}). You may stop working at any time.
  2. Consume and save: Allocate your available resources between consumption and saving. What you save becomes your capital for the next period.

How the economy works

Output is produced using the technology Y = {{ C.A | fmtnum(places=0) }} × K1/3 × L2/3, where K is aggregate capital and L is aggregate labor across all {{ C.GROUP_SIZE }} participants.

Prices equal marginal products: the wage and the rental rate are determined by how much capital and labor the economy has. If either aggregate capital or aggregate labor is zero, the economy shuts down for that period (no output, no income).

You start with {{ C.K_BAR | fmtnum(places=0) }} units of capital.

Redemption value

Your earnings each period equal the redemption value of your consumption, given by u(c) = 2√c. Because this function is concave, consuming more in a single period yields diminishing returns. This creates an incentive to smooth consumption across periods.

Continuation

After each period, there is a {{ (player.context.settings.beta * 100) | fmtnum(places=0) }}% chance that the economy continues to the next period. When the economy ends, you are paid the sum of your redemption values across all periods.
