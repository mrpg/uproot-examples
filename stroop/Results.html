The first measure of interference from the word meaning is the difference between the share of correct responses in congruent trials and the share of correct responses in incongruent trials. A larger share of correct responses in congruent trials—that is, a larger share of errors in incongruent trials—indicates interference from the word meaning.

The Stroop effect is the difference between the mean response time for correct responses in incongruent trials and the mean response time for correct responses in congruent trials. A positive value indicates interference from the word meaning.
